# Method 01 — Oblique Strategies (侧向思维策略)

**Source**: Brian Eno & Peter Schmidt, 1975. 一套印在卡片上的短句，每张一句看似不相关的话，帮你从卡死的地方跳出来。https://www.enoshop.co.uk/product/oblique-strategies/

**Originator**: Brian Eno (英国音乐人/制作人，U2、Talking Heads、Bowie 的制作人) + Peter Schmidt (德国画家)

## What it is
A deck of ~115 cards, each with a one-line aphorism or instruction. Draw one at random when stuck. The card is not a literal instruction — it's a deflection from your current thought path.

## Famous examples
- "Repetition is a form of change"
- "Emphasize the flaws"
- "Work at a different speed"
- "What would your closest friend do?"
- "Honor thy error as a hidden intention"
- "Only a part, not the whole"
- "Make a blank valuable by putting it in an exquisite frame"
- "Overtly resist change"

## How to use this method in creative-ideation
1. When user says "卡住了"/"推我一把"/"没灵感" → draw 1-3 cards at random
2. Don't explain the card — apply it literally to their problem for 1 cycle
3. After 1 cycle, ask: did anything shift? If no, draw again

## When NOT to use
- User has a concrete decision (use Pre-mortem instead)
- User needs divergent volume (use SCAMPER instead)
- User wants to refine existing work (use Defamiliarization instead)

## Output shape
For each drawn card:
- **Card**: [the aphorism verbatim]
- **Applied to your [problem]**: [literal interpretation in their context]
- **What this forces**: [the cognitive move the card makes you do]
