# Method 07 — Biomimicry (仿生学)

**Source**: Janine Benyus, 1997, *Biomimicry: Innovation Inspired by Nature*。https://biomimicry.net/

**Originator**: Janine Benyus (美国作家, Biomimicry Institute 创始人)

## What it is
Look at how nature has solved an analogous problem, then transfer the strategy. Nature has 3.8 billion years of R&D on constraint sets that often parallel engineering problems.

## Core move
1. State the function you need, not the form: "I need to move fluid with no pump" not "I need a pump"
2. Search for biological analogues: what organisms solve the same function?
3. Identify the mechanism, abstract it
4. Apply the abstracted mechanism to your problem

## Famous examples
- **Velcro** → 苍耳属 (Arctium) 种子钩 (1941, de Mestral)
- **Shinkansen 500 系列鼻锥** → 翠鸟入水 (无冲击波, 1997)
- **Eastgate Centre 温控** → 白蚁巢 (被动恒温, 1996)
- **鲨鱼皮泳衣** → 鲨鱼皮微结构 (2008)
- **自洁表面** → 荷叶超疏水微结构 (Barthlott, 1990s)

## How to use this method in creative-ideation
1. User has a function problem: "我需要一个 [X]"
2. Reframe as: "自然界怎么实现 [function 描述]？"
3. Search the biological strategies database: asknature.org
4. Pick 2-3 analogues, abstract the mechanism, transfer

## Best for
- 工程/物理类问题 (结构、流体、热、动)
- 用户体验设计 (蜂群、自组织)
- 永续/低能耗方案
- 形态/材料创新

## When NOT to use
- 纯语言/写作问题
- 商业策略问题 (自然界不直接对应)
- 时间紧迫 — research 阶段耗时

## Output shape
- **Function needed**: [abstract function, not form]
- **Biological analogue 1**: [organism + mechanism] → **Abstracted principle**: [the transfer]
- **Biological analogue 2**: ...
- **Transfer to your problem**: [the concrete application]
