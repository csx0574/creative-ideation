# Method 21 — Time Travel Ideation (时间穿越思考)

**Source**: 现代设计/写作工具，源自 speculative design (Bruce Sterling, Anthony Dunne, Fiona Raby, 1990s-2000s)。

**Originator**: Anthony Dunne & Fiona Raby (英国设计批评家, "Speculative Design" 概念创造者)

## What it is
Frame the problem in a different time. Not just "future" — specific futures, and also pasts, and the present from a stranger's perspective.

## Variants
- **Future forward**: "It's 2040. We're looking back at how this problem got solved. What happened?"
- **Future backward**: "It's 2040. We're looking back at a *failure* to solve this. What went wrong?"
- **Past forward**: "It's 1900. A person from that era reads our spec. What do they misunderstand?"
- **Past backward**: "It's 1900. This problem was already solved (or dissolved) — how?"
- **Present alien**: "An alien anthropologist observes your project today. What do they see that you don't?"

## How to use this method in creative-ideation
1. User is stuck in present-moment thinking
2. Force a 50-year time jump (forward or backward)
3. The gap reveals what the user is *assuming* is permanent
4. Often: trends are temporary, the fundamental human need is permanent, the *solution shape* is what changes

## Best for
- 长期项目
- "我的方案 5 年后还有用吗"
- 战略/产品规划
- 写未来题材内容

## When NOT to use
- 短平快交付
- 紧急修复
- 用户没耐心做思考

## Output shape
- **Time frame**: [year + viewpoint]
- **Observation from that time**: [what they'd see]
- **What looks permanent now but won't be**: [the assumption that dissolves]
- **What looks temporary now but won't be**: [the assumption that strengthens]
- **Implication for the work now**: [the move to make today]
