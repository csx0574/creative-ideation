# Method 12 — Affinity Diagrams (亲和图)

**Source**: Jiro Kawakita, 1960s, 川喜田二郎 (日本人類学者, 整理登山观察笔记时发明)。https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affinity_diagram

**Originator**: Jiro Kawakita (川喜田二郎, 1920-2009)

## What it is
A way to find structure in a large pile of qualitative observations (notes, quotes, facts) by clustering them into natural groups, then naming the groups. It's the qualitative version of factor analysis.

## The move
1. Get a pile of raw observations on sticky notes / cards (ideally 30-100)
2. Without trying to organize, look for notes that *feel* related
3. Cluster them
4. Once clustered, name each cluster with a short phrase
5. The names often reveal the underlying structure of the problem space

## How to use this method in creative-ideation
1. User has a vague sense of "lots of stuff matters" — research notes, user complaints, scattered thoughts
2. Dump it all as discrete items
3. Cluster silently
4. Name clusters: 3-7 names usually emerges
5. The names become the *frame* of the solution

## Best for
- 用户研究 (整理访谈/反馈)
- 大问题分解
- 团队共识形成
- 复杂多因素决策

## When NOT to use
- 单一明确问题 (overkill)
- 需要快速决定
- 数据点少于 ~10 (用 Brainstorming)

## Famous variant
**KJ Method** (named after Kawakita Jiro): the full method includes making a narrative diagram with super-clusters and arrows showing causal flow.

## Output shape
- **Raw observations**: [list of N items]
- **Clusters (silently formed)**: [3-7 groups]
- **Cluster names**: [short phrase per group]
- **Insight from naming**: [what the names reveal about the problem's structure]
