# Method 20 — Role Storming (角色风暴)

**Source**: Rick Griggs, 1980s, 文献 "Role Storming" — 经典 Brainstorming 变体。https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brainstorming#Role_storming

**Originator**: Rick Griggs (加州圣何塞州立大学)

## What it is
Brainstorm, but first you adopt the role of an *unrelated* person. "How would a 5-year-old solve this? An astronaut? My grandmother? A medieval monk? A bored CEO?" The role change unlocks different language and constraints.

## Why it works
When you're yourself, you self-censor. When you're a persona, you have permission to think differently. Plus, the role's *domain knowledge* (what a 5-year-old knows about) is genuinely different from yours, and that's the source of novelty.

## How to use this method in creative-ideation
1. Pick 3-5 roles wildly unrelated to the user's domain
2. For each role, *in their voice*, answer the user's question
3. Discard the parts that don't fit
4. The remaining ideas have a DNA signature of the role — often exactly the "new" the user wanted

## Best for
- 创意 + 多角度
- "想换个角度看看"
- 团队/客户陈述 (audience-shifting)
- Marketing copy variations

## When NOT to use
- 严肃技术问题
- 用户焦虑 (角色扮演可能加重)
- 决策 (用 Pre-mortem)

## Roles to try
- 5-year-old child
- Grandmother
- The user themselves, 10 years in the future
- A medieval monk
- A bored CEO
- A chef
- A stunt double
- A librarian
- A ghost
- The user themselves, on the day they gave up on this project

## Output shape
For each role:
- **As [role], I would**: [in their voice, addressing the user's problem]
- **What this role knows that the user doesn't**: [the unique angle]
- **Steal-worthy idea**: [the part to keep]
