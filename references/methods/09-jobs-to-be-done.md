# Method 09 — Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

**Source**: Clayton Christensen (哈佛商学院, "Competing Against Luck" 2016) + 早期理论来自 Theodore Levitt (1960s) + 销售数据积累。

**Originator**: Clayton Christensen (1952-2020) + Bob Moesta

## What it is
Customers don't buy products — they "hire" products to do a *job* in their life. A milkshake isn't a drink, it's hired to (a) fill the morning commute, (b) give hands something to do, (c) keep the kids quiet in the car. Different jobs → different product specs.

## Core move
1. Forget the product. Ask: "What job was the customer *firing* the old solution to do? What job is the new solution *hiring* them to do?"
2. Identify the *progress* the customer is trying to make (functional + emotional + social)
3. Map all the "hires" and "fires" across the journey
4. The product is whatever best does the job; competitors are *all* the things that could be hired (including doing nothing)

## How to use this method in creative-ideation
1. User wants to make/build a product/feature/essay
2. Reframe: "用户雇这个来完成什么 progress? 雇佣前在用什么? 为什么换/不换?"
3. Often reveals: the real competition is "用户什么都不做" or "用户用 Notion/Excel 做"
4. Reframe spec based on the actual job

## Best for
- 产品定位
- 新功能 spec
- 营销文案/landing page 角度
- 解释"为什么我的 X 没人用"

## When NOT to use
- 纯创作 (写作、艺术) — 自我表达无"job"
- 现有功能优化 (SCAMPER 更直接)
- 用户还没确定目标人群

## Famous example
McDonald's milkshake 研究 (Bob Moesta, Christensen 团队): 早高峰买的奶昔是被"雇"来填饱 + 打发开车无聊 + 不弄脏手 的。竞争对手不是其他奶昔，是香蕉、Bagel、"什么都不买"。

## Output shape
- **Product/feature**: [name]
- **Job (functional)**: [what progress]
- **Job (emotional)**: [how they want to feel]
- **Job (social)**: [how they want to be seen]
- **Currently hired to do this job**: [the incumbent, often surprising]
- **Implication for design**: [what the spec should change to]
